Józef Piłsudzki

About Józef Piłsudzki

Józef Piłsudski was a Polish statesman who is considered the father of the Second Polish Republic. He was a soldier, politician and statesman. He was the Chief of State (1918–22) and First Marshal of Poland (from 1920). He was the leader of the Second Polish Republic from 1918 to 1922 and de facto leader from 1926 to 1935. He was the leader of the Polish Legions during the First World War and regained Polish independence in 1918. He was the Marshal of Poland from 1920 to 1923 and the de facto dictator of Poland from 1926 to 1935. He was the founder of the Sanation movement. He was born in Zalavas, Lithuania, and died in Warsaw, Poland.

Here's a time line of Józef Piłsudzki's life:

  • 1867 - Born in Zalavas, Lithuania.
  • 1887-1892 - Exiled to Siberia
  • 1914 - Leads the Polish Legions in World War I.
  • 1918 - Regains Polish independence after World War I.
  • 1920 - Leads the Polish-Soviet War.
  • 1926 - Becomes a de facto dictator of Poland.
  • 1935 - Dies in Warsaw, Poland.

Key Achievements

  • Establishment of the Second Polish Republic
  • Leadership during the Polish-Soviet War
  • Modernization of the Polish Army
